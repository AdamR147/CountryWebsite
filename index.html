Santo Domingo

Santo Domingo, the vibrant capital of the Dominican Republic, is a city where rich history and modern Caribbean life blend seamlessly. Founded in 1496, it is the oldest continuosly inhabited European settlement in the Americas. It is home to the Colonial Zone, a UNESCO World Heritage Site filled with cobblestone streets, centurie-old churches, and the first cathedral of the New World. Beyond its historical site, the city pulses with lively music, flavorful cuisine, and warm, welcoming locals. From its thriving arts and nightlife scenes, Santo Domingo offers a dynamic mix of tradition and innovation that captures the spirit of the Dominican Republic.

Cibao Norte

Cibao Norte is a vibrant subregion of the Dominican Republic known for its rich agricultural production, dynamic urban centers, and strong cultural heritage. Anchored by cities such as Santiago which is often considered as the nation's second capital. Cibao Norte blends modern industry with rural life. Fertile valleys support the cultivation of tobacco, coffee, and other key crops that fuel both local livelihoods and national exports. Cibao Norte is also recognized for its music, festivals, and other entrepreneurial, making it a center of economic and cultural energy.
