Dominican Cuisine

The most popular Dominican Food is La Bandera Dominicana, meaning the Dominican Flag. It is the national dish which features a hearty lunch plate of white rice, red beans, and stewed meat, usually chicken or beef which represent the colors of the Dominican flag, hence the name. La Bandera Dominicana is usually served with sides like salad, fried plantains, & avocado. La Bandera Dominicana is a staple of Dominican cuisine as it is deeply rooted in the country's daily life and history. Another popular dish is Mofongo, a dish that has fried mashed green plantains, mixed with garlic, olive oil, and pork. It can also be served with a broth (caldo) which has chicken or beef and is poured over the Mofongo, enhancing the flavor and adding moisture. Another dish that is widely loved by Dominicans is Mangu. Mangu is a Dominican breakfast dish made from boiled down green plantains and is mashed with butter or olive oil and typically topped with red onions. It is a staple of Dominican cuisine and often served with fried eggs, fried cheese, & salami. Next, another common Dominican food is Sancocho which is often considered the national dish and a primary culinary treasure in Dominican Republic and seen as the "king" of Dominican dishes and usually traditionally reserved for special occasions. Sancocho is a rich, hearty meat and vegetable stew and also has several variations to it such as, Sancocho de Siete Carnes, a seven-meat Sancocho featuring, chicken, pork, beef, sausage, smoked ham, pork chops, & ham. Habichuelas con Dulce is another dish widely adored by Dominicans as it is a traditional dessert, which has a creamy rich soup kind of consistency. Habichuelas con Dulce is made from pureed red kidney beans, coconut milk, evaporated milk, sweet potatoes, raisins, warm spices such as cloves & cinnamon, and often served with cookies, or cassabe (toasted cassava bread) The dish is especially popular during holidays like Lent and Easter. Habichuelas con Dulce is a deeply cultural dish, symbolizing, tradition, and family. These are just some of the many foods Dominican Republic has to offer and there is so much more and they each hold a strong traditional value among Dominicans.
