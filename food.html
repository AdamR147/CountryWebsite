Dominican Cuisine

Sancocho

Sancocho, is a hearty traditional stew enjoyed across several Latin American and Caribbean countries, each adding their own flair to the dish. Typically made with a rich broth, starchy vegetables like yucca, plantains, and potatoes. It includes meats such as chicken, beef, pork, and sometimes even fish. Sancocho is known for its comforting, soul-warming qualities. Slow-cooked which allows the flavors to meld together, and its often served with rice, which makes it both filling and flavorful. Sancocho is more than just a meal, it is the center of happy times such as family gathering, celebrations, symobolizing community, and warmth.

Mangu

Mangu is a beloved Dominican dish made from boiled down and mashed green plantains, known for its smooth creamy texture and comforting flavors. Mangu is typically served as part of a traditional breakfast called "los tres golpes," it's paired with fried cheese, salami, and eggs, which creates a satisfying meal. The dish has roots in African culinary traditions, reflecting the island's cultural heritage and long-standing appreciation for plantains. Whether it is enjoyed at home or in local eateries, Mangu is more than just food but rather a symbol for Dominican identity, warmth, and community.
